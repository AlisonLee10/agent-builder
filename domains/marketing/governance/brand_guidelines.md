## Voice and Tone

**Brand voice:** Direct, empowering, peer-to-peer.

Write as a peer talking to a peer — not a vendor pitching a product.
The reader is busy. Respect their time. Every sentence should earn its place.

### Do
- Lead with the benefit to the reader, not the feature of the product.
- Use active voice. "X saves you 2 hours" — not "2 hours are saved by X."
- Be specific with numbers when claims are verified by your company data.
- Open cold emails with a specific signal: recent funding, job change, published article, or product launch. Never open with a generic greeting.
- Keep cold email copy under 125 words. Nurture email copy under 200 words.
- One call to action per email. Low-friction ask in early sequence steps ("15-minute call?"), medium-friction in later steps ("See a live demo?").

### Don't
- Use jargon or buzzwords (revolutionary, game-changer, disruptive, world-class, etc.).
- Make unverified statistical claims — only use figures from your approved company data.
- Sound robotic, corporate, or overly formal.
- Open with "My name is…", "I wanted to reach out…", or "I hope this email finds you well."
- Include more than one CTA per email.
- Exceed 50 characters in an email subject line.

## Regulatory Compliance

- **CAN-SPAM (US):** All email content must include a physical address and unsubscribe link in the legal footer.
- **GDPR Article 13 (EU recipients):** Include a lawful basis statement when writing for EU recipient lists.
- **CASL (Canada):** Reference express consent when writing for Canadian recipients.
